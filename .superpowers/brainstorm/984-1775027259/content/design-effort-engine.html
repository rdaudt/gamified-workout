The effort engine — how the score is calculated

Four factors that feed the effort score
35%
weight
Journey stage
How far into this exercise stream are you? Early sessions improve fast — the body adapts. Later sessions are harder to move. The engine knows where you are and adjusts expectations accordingly.
Example: Session 3 with +40% is expected. Session 40 with +5% is exceptional.
30%
weight
Plateau history
How long has it been since you last improved on this exercise? Every failed session before today builds pressure. Breaking a 4-session plateau scores far higher than improving on your first try.
Example: Stuck at 17 push-ups for 3 sessions. Today: 18. That single rep is worth more than going 10→17 in session 2.
20%
weight
Consistency
Are you showing up regularly? A streak of consistent sessions — even without improvement — means the effort is real and sustained. Consistency also gets its own card type when the number doesn't move.
Example: 8 sessions in 10 days with no rep gain still scores a 52 — because showing up is the work.
15%
weight
Form quality
Did the reps count fully? 20 reps with 90% good form scores higher than 20 reps with 50% good form. Consistently improving form — even at the same rep count — is recognised as progress.
Example: Same 15 reps as last time, but form score went from 60% → 85%. Card acknowledges the quality improvement.
