Product Design — Section 1

What we're building and why it works

The product
Beat Past You
Working title
A free web app that uses your camera to count reps, track your personal history across exercise types, and generate a shareable performance card after every session — with automated form feedback and a natural path to personal training.
Three jobs this product does
📣
Acquisition
Every shared card carries your brand to strangers. Free tool, zero friction, viral potential. People who find it become leads.
🔁
Retention
Streaks, history, and the "beat past you" mechanic keep people coming back. Clients stay engaged between sessions with you.
🎯
Conversion
Form flags create warm leads. "Your squat depth has been dropping — book a free assessment with [Your Name]." App does the screening; you close.
How a stranger becomes your client
1
Sees a shared card on social media
A friend posts their "+38% push-ups" card. Your branding is on it. They click the link.
Discovery
2
Tries the app as a guest — no signup
Does one workout. Sees the live skeleton, hears the rep count, gets a card. Hooked.
Trial
3
Creates a free account to save their history
"Save this workout and start tracking your progress." Now they're a user. History builds. Streak starts.
Activation
4
App flags a form issue
"Your lower back is losing tension on push-ups after rep 12. This won't improve without coaching."
Warm lead
5
Books a free assessment with you
CTA embedded in the form feedback. They arrive already knowing what the problem is. You show them the fix. They sign up.
Client
What this is — and isn't
✕
Not a subscription fitness app competing with Peloton or Nike Training
✕
Not a video storage platform — no recordings are ever kept
✕
Not a replacement for a trainer — it explicitly says so and points to you
✓
A free tool that does something genuinely useful, carries your brand, and feeds your practice
