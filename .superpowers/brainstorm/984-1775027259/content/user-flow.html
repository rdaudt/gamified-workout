App User Flow

From opening the app to sharing the card

① Setup
1
Open app — log in or continue as guest
Logged-in users get history tracking and cards. Guests can try the experience once, then prompted to create a free account to save.
Identity
2
Select exercise type
Push-ups, squats, crunches, burpees, lunges… Each exercise is a separate tracking stream. Pick one for this session.
Exercise stream
3
Position device — alignment guide shown
On-screen skeleton guide helps the user get the right angle. App confirms it can see the full body before proceeding.
Camera check
② Live session
4
User says "Start"
Voice command activates. No need to touch the screen. Countdown overlay (3… 2… 1…) then live detection begins.
"START"
5
Live rep counting with skeleton overlay
AI tracks body pose frame by frame. Each completed rep increments the counter. Running total visible on screen. No video is recorded — only rep events are logged.
LIVE · 12 reps
6
User says "Done"
Voice command stops the session. Rep count is locked. Camera feed stops immediately.
"DONE"
③ Analysis
7
Instant performance analysis
App compares today's result against full history for this exercise. Calculates effort score — factoring in plateau length, consistency streak, and where you are in your journey. Not just the raw number.
Effort-aware engine
8
Quick report + shareable card shown
Screen shows: today's count, vs last time, effort score, streak, and a generated "past self" quote calibrated to the context. Card preview shown below the report.
Card generated
④ Save or discard
9
User chooses: Save or Discard
If the session was a warmup, a mistake, or just not representative — they can discard. No penalty. Only confirmed sessions enter the history.
Save workoutData stored. Card unlocked for sharing.
DiscardNothing saved. No trace. Try again.
10
Card available to share
Download as image or share directly. Card carries your trainer's branding. Every share is an acquisition touchpoint.
What gets saved: exercise type · date · rep count · duration · effort score. What never gets saved: video, camera feed, or any recording.
Note on form quality: When should a half-rep count? The app can flag questionable reps separately (e.g. "17 full reps · 4 partial"). You decide as the trainer what the threshold is per exercise. This is worth deciding before building — it's a design choice, not a technical one.
